About Me

I am Khushi Jindal, currently pursuing a Master’s in Computer Science and Engineering with a specialization in Artificial Intelligence at the Indira Gandhi Delhi Technical University for Women (IGDTUW), Delhi. Currently conducting a Master’s dissertation at the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research - Institute of Genomics and Integrative Biology (CSIR-IGIB), under the guidance of Dr. Jitendra Narayan and Dr. D.K. Tayal. The research focuses on leveraging interpretable machine learning and explainable AI techniques to create a robust framework for Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) detection, bridging computational biology and AI to provide actionable insights for early diagnosis and enhance trust in AI-driven healthcare solutions.

During a summer internship at Indira Gandhi Delhi Technical University for Women (IGDTUW), under the guidance of Dr. Seeja K.R., I worked on Alzheimer’s disease detection using MRI imaging. The project involved designing a deep learning pipeline with various advanced architectures to enhance diagnostic accuracy. Additionally, I conducted comprehensive research on deep learning techniques, culminating in a review paper. This work focused on enabling early detection and improving diagnostic precision for Alzheimer’s disease, contributing to advancements in medical imaging and healthcare solutions.

I completed my Bachelor's from GGSIPU University, Delhi in Computer Science and Engineering. I worked on enhancing ATM transaction security using dual authentication with face detection and PIN verification and interned at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where I developed a biometric recognition system for secure and efficient attendance logging using deep learning techniques.

Research Interests: My research focuses on the intersection of computer vision and healthcare, aiming to develop autonomous diagnostic systems that integrate diverse data modalities to predict disease progression, identify treatments, and improve patient outcomes. I emphasize explainability and edge-device applicability, in AI, machine learning, and deep learning for medical imaging/signal analysis, and computational biology.

Selected Publications

Enhancing Autism Spectrum Disorder Using Classification and Ensemble Approaches: A Study on Hyperparameter Tuning and Outlier Detection

Khushi Jindal*, D.K. Tayal, Jitendra Narayan

Submitted

This study introduces an Outlier-Driven Prediction Framework that combines machine learning models with outlier detection and hyperparameter tuning to improve ASD detection. The performance of Decision Trees, Random Forests, Gaussian Naive Bayes, and Extra Trees was evaluated on high-dimensional, imbalanced clinical datasets. The local outlier factor (LOF) method was used for robust outlier detection. The study, using a dataset from the UCI ML Repository focused on adult ASD patients, achieved 98.97% accuracy with the Extra Trees classifier.

Advancements in Alzheimer's Disease Detection: A Comprehensive Review of Deep Learning Approaches in MRI Imaging

Khushi Jindal*,Seeja. K.R, and D.K. Tayal

Accepted

International Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Speech Technology (AIST)’24

This study explores deep learning techniques for detecting Alzheimer’s disease, including CNNs, hybrid models, ensemble methods, and transfer learning. CNNs are central in analyzing MRI images, while hybrid and ensemble models improve accuracy and robustness by combining algorithms. Transfer learning using pre-trained models enhances diagnosis, especially with limited data. Additionally, novel technologies like transformers and multi-stage frameworks complement existing methods. The research demonstrates that these integrated approaches significantly improve detection accuracy and early diagnosis, highlighting the impact of advanced deep learning technologies.

Harnessing AWS for Transaction Monitoring: A Comprehensive Study on Cloud-Based Anomaly Detection

Khushi Jindal*, Kusum Sharma, Muskan Tomar, SRN Reddy

Paper

International Journal of Innovative Science and Research Technology

This study proposes an integrated system for supervising e-transactions and detecting fraud using supervised learning techniques on Amazon SageMaker. By addressing class imbalance through weighted adjustments and synthetic data generation, and optimizing hyperparameters, the system enhances performance. Among the models tested, Random Forest proved to be the most accurate, achieving 99.98% accuracy, 99.97% precision, and 100% recall in detecting fraudulent transactions. The results highlight the effectiveness of AI cloud-based solutions like SageMaker in improving bank security against cyber threats.

Securing ATM Transactions Using Double Authentication

Khushi Jindal*, Nimit Jain, Muskan Tomar, SRN Reddy

Paper

International Conference on Data Analytics & Management (ICDAM)’ 2023

This study presents the development of a deep learning-based ATM transaction system designed to enhance security through real-time biometric authentication via face detection. By integrating dual authentication methods—facial recognition and PIN verification—the system creates a robust security model that effectively prevents unauthorized access and reduces the risk of fraud. Additionally, a secure database has been implemented to store user information and transaction logs, ensuring data integrity and compliance with privacy standards. This setup not only strengthens security but also facilitates efficient transaction management.
